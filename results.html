Firesetting Milestones Exploratory Survey

Conducted 2024 by Adrian Seeley (contact@adrianseeley.com)

Abstract

This anonymous self-report study explores the correlations between firesetting milestones in a convenience sample of 214 volunteers recruited from SurveyCircle between Q3-Q4 2024. The questionnaire captured 52 yes/no questions corresponding to whether or not they have experienced a given milestone. No identifying information such as age, sex, or location was collected to reduce self-incrimination bias. Analysis included measurement of prevalence, conditional prevalence, and condiditional prevalence delta (i.e., those who X were more likely to Y). Non-parametric bootstrap resampling with replacement (b=1000) was used to calculate confidence intervals for each statistic. Seven key findings are presented:

  • 1. Received fire safety training may increase the prevalence of Hurt myself with fire, and Hurt someone else with fire.
  • 2. Received fire safety training may increase the prevalence of Started a fire intentionally, Started a fire accidently, Started a small fire, Started an average size fire, Started a large fire, and Started an extremely large fire.
  • 3. Received fire safety training may decrease the prevalence of Lit fireworks.
  • 4. Received fire safety training may have no impact on Been in trouble because of fire, Started an extremely large fire, Used fire as a cry for help, Hurt someone else with fire, Lied about a fire I started, Used fire to destroy or conceal evidence of a crime, Been in legal or criminal trouble because of a fire, Used fire to manipulate or control someone, Used fire to get attention, Set a fire to get revenge, and Used fire as a weapon.
  • 5. Pathways leading towards Had a fire get out of control may include Burned leaves and/or brush, Started a fire with friends, Used an accelerant such as gasoline or lighter fluid to start a fire, Started a fire alone, Burned garbage, Thrown or placed dangerous items into a fire, and Worked as a welder.
  • 6. There may be a constellation of co-occurence including Started a fire accidently, Accidently started a fire in the house, Tried to hide a fire that I started, Attempted to conceal a fire, Lied about a fire I started, and Been in trouble because of fire.
  • 7. There may be a constellation of co-occurence including Used fire as a cry for help, Used fire to destroy or conceal evidence of a crime, Been in legal or criminal trouble because of a fire, Used fire to manipulate or control someone, Used fire to get attention, Set a fire to get revenge, and Used fire as a weapon; Notably, no other milestones revealed significant differention of pathways leading towards aberrant milestones.
